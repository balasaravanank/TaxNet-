Compiled from DGGI enforcement actions, CBIC press releases, and court records (2019–2024)
For training and reference purposes only — entity names anonymized.

CASE 001 — CIRCULAR TRADING RING, DELHI NCR, 2022
Fraud Type: Circular Invoice Trading (Carousel)
Entities Involved: 6 companies (Ring A→B→C→D→E→F→A)
Total Cycling Value: Rs. 47.3 Crore
ITC Fraudulently Availed: Rs. 8.5 Crore
Detection Method: Graph cycle analysis — all 6 entities had invoiced each other in a closed loop within 3 months of registration

KEY FRAUD SIGNALS DETECTED:
  - All 6 GSTINs registered within 45 days of each other
  - Common directors across 3 of the 6 entities
  - Invoice amounts: Rs. 7–9 Crore per chain link (unusually high for the stated business type)
  - No e-way bills found for any of the claimed goods movements
  - Bank account analysis: money returned to originating account within 7–10 days of each transaction
  - GSTR-3B filed for only 2 months, then all 6 entities stopped filing

ENFORCEMENT OUTCOME:
  - All 6 GSTINs cancelled under Section 29(2)(e)
  - ITC blocked under Rule 86A within 48 hours of detection
  - FIR lodged; 3 directors arrested

LESSON FOR DETECTION SYSTEMS: Short cycle length (6 entities), high per-transaction value, low entity age, and coordinated filing cessation are the strongest combined signals.

CASE 002 — SHELL COMPANY NETWORK, MUMBAI, 2023
Fraud Type: Shell Company / Ghost Firm ITC Harvesting
Entities Involved: 8 shell GSTINs supply chain fraudulently feeding 25 real buyer companies
Total ITC Fraudulently Passed: Rs. 23.8 Crore
Detection Method: In-degree/out-degree anomaly — shells had high in-degree (from many real companies) but zero out-degree (never actually sold to anyone downstream)

KEY FRAUD SIGNALS DETECTED:
  - Shell companies: registered address was a single room; no goods inventory or warehouse
  - Each shell issued invoices to 8–12 real buyer companies
  - Each shell had zero outward supply (never sold goods to anyone)
  - High invoice frequency in a single month (30–50 invoices in one month, then zero)
  - Phone numbers and emails used in registration were disposable/pre-paid

ENFORCEMENT OUTCOME:
  - Rs. 15 Crore recovered through bank account provisional attachment
  - 8 beneficial owners identified through PAN linkage
  - All 25 buyer companies issued demand notices for reversal of wrongly availed ITC

LESSON FOR DETECTION SYSTEMS: Shell companies always exhibit the pattern: IN-DEGREE >> OUT-DEGREE, with in-weight (incoming transaction value) far exceeding out-weight (outgoing). The ratio of inflow to outflow approaching infinity is the defining characteristic.

CASE 003 — TAX MISMATCH / ITC INFLATION, SURAT (GUJARAT), 2021
Fraud Type: ITC amount inflation — claiming more ITC than supplier actually paid
Entities Involved: 12 companies in textile and diamond trading
Total ITC Over-Claimed: Rs. 31.2 Crore
Detection Method: GSTR-2A mismatch — ITC claimed in GSTR-3B significantly exceeded tax reflected in GSTR-2A (counter-party filings)

KEY FRAUD SIGNALS DETECTED:
  - ITC claimed: Rs. 31.2 Crore
  - ITC eligible (verified from supplier GSTR-3B): Rs. 4.8 Crore
  - Mismatch: Rs. 26.4 Crore (81% of claimed ITC was fraudulent)
  - Tax mismatch ratio: 6.5x (claimed 6.5 times more than eligible)
  - All inflated invoices were in the same 2-month period before financial year end

ENFORCEMENT OUTCOME:
  - Demand notices issued for Rs. 26.4 Crore + 18% interest + 100% penalty = Rs. 60+ Crore total liability
  - 2 managing directors arrested under Section 132

LESSON FOR DETECTION SYSTEMS: The ratio of ITC_claimed / ITC_eligible > 1.5 is a strong red flag. Ratio > 3.0 is near-certain fraud. Values approaching 5.0 or above indicate deliberate systematic inflation.

CASE 004 — VOLUME SPIKE FRAUD, BENGALURU (KARNATAKA), 2023
Fraud Type: Last-minute ITC harvest before financial year closure
Entities Involved: 4 companies (IT services, consulting)
Total ITC Fraudulently Availed: Rs. 8.7 Crore

KEY FRAUD SIGNALS DETECTED:
  - Normal months: 2–5 invoices per month, average Rs. 5 Lakh per invoice
  - February/March 2023 (year-end): 78 invoices, average Rs. 1.2 Crore per invoice
  - Volume spike ratio: 1,400% increase in transaction value over previous months
  - All year-end invoices from the same 3 supplier GSTINs (concentrated, not diversified)
  - None of the year-end suppliers had e-way bills or physical delivery documentation

ENFORCEMENT OUTCOME:
  - Year-end ITC blocked under Rule 86A
  - Scrutiny assessment issued under Section 61

LESSON FOR DETECTION SYSTEMS: Volume spike detection must compare each entity's current period transaction volume against its own historical baseline. A Z-Score > 3.0 on monthly transaction count relative to that entity's own average is a strong fraud signal, especially when the spike occurs in February/March (Indian financial year end).

CASE 005 — DUPLICATE INVOICE FRAUD, HYDERABAD (TELANGANA), 2022
Fraud Type: Same invoices claimed multiple times / invoice re-use
Entities Involved: 2 companies (buyer-seller pair)
Total Fraudulent ITC: Rs. 2.1 Crore

KEY FRAUD SIGNALS DETECTED:
  - Same invoice number, same amount, same date filed in 3 consecutive GSTR-3B periods
  - Invoice amount: Rs. 70 Lakh — filed in April, May, and June 2022
  - Same GSTIN pair (seller: 36XXXXX, buyer: 36YYYYY) appearing in 3 months

ENFORCEMENT OUTCOME:
  - Rs. 2.1 Crore demand + penalty
  - Amendment to GSTR-3B required by buyer

LESSON FOR DETECTION SYSTEMS: Duplicate detection should check for (seller_gstin, buyer_gstin, invoice_amount, invoice_date) tuples appearing more than once. Even a single duplicate in this signature almost always indicates fraudulent re-use of an invoice.

CASE 006 — MIXED FRAUD RING WITH PROFESSIONAL FACILITATION, CHENNAI, 2024
Fraud Type: Combination — circular trading + shell companies + professional enablers
Entities Involved: 18 companies across Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh
Professional Enablers: 3 CA firms found to have signed fraudulent GST returns
Total Fraud: Rs. 89 Crore

KEY FRAUD SIGNALS DETECTED:
  - 4 circular trading rings nested within a larger network
  - Rings: sizes 3, 4, 5, 6 entities respectively
  - All rings interconnected at 2 common hub entities (betweenness centrality >> 0.8)
  - Hub entities had the highest PageRank in the network
  - Total cycling value across all rings: Rs. 89 Crore
  - Registration dates: all 18 GSTINs registered within 90 days

ENFORCEMENT OUTCOME:
  - Largest GST fraud ring busted in Tamil Nadu as of 2024
  - Bar Council complaint filed against the 3 enabling CA firms
  - 7 arrests

LESSON FOR DETECTION SYSTEMS: Hub entities (high betweenness centrality + high PageRank) that appear in multiple rings simultaneously are the most dangerous actors in a fraud network. PageRank anomaly scoring effectively identifies these "super-connectors" who bridge multiple fraud rings.
