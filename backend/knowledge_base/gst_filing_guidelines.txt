GST RETURN FILING GUIDELINES AND CROSS-VALIDATION RULES
Source: GSTN Portal Guidelines, CBIC FAQ, GST Act Rule 59–84

---

GSTR-1: OUTWARD SUPPLIES RETURN

WHAT IT CONTAINS:
  - Every invoice issued by a registered supplier during the tax period
  - Invoice details: GSTIN of buyer, invoice number, date, taxable value, integrated tax/central tax/state tax amounts
  - Debit notes and credit notes issued
  - Advances received for future supply
  - Details of exports and nil-rated/exempt supplies

FILING FREQUENCY:
  - Monthly (if aggregate turnover > Rs. 5 Crore in previous FY)
  - Quarterly under QRMP scheme (if aggregate turnover ≤ Rs. 5 Crore)

DUE DATE: 11th of the month following the tax period (monthly filers)

FRAUD DETECTION RELEVANCE:
  - A supplier's GSTR-1 creates the GSTR-2A/2B (auto-populated purchase register) for their buyer
  - If a supplier files GSTR-1 but does NOT remit the tax in GSTR-3B, the buyer's ITC credit is uncovered
  - High discrepancy between filed GSTR-1 value and actual GSTR-3B tax payment = Warning signal
  - GSTR-1 filed with unusually large invoices to a single buyer repeatedly = Concentrated fraud signal

---

GSTR-3B: SUMMARY RETURN

WHAT IT CONTAINS:
  - Total outward taxable supplies (sales)
  - Interstate / Intrastate breakup
  - Exempt/nil-rated/non-GST supplies
  - Total ITC available (from GSTR-2A/2B)
  - ITC claimed (may be less than eligible)
  - Net tax payable (sales tax minus ITC)
  - Actual cash payment made to government

FILING FREQUENCY: Monthly (due 20th of following month)

KEY CROSS-VALIDATION RULE — GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B:
  - Output tax in supplier's GSTR-3B must match (or exceed) the output tax in their GSTR-1
  - A supplier with GSTR-1 showing Rs. 1 Crore output tax but GSTR-3B showing only Rs. 10 Lakh payment is in violation
  - The gap (Rs. 90 Lakh) represents uncovered ITC — if a buyer claimed this, their ITC is fraudulent

KEY CROSS-VALIDATION RULE — ITC CLAIM vs GSTR-2B:
  - ITC claimed must not exceed ITC reflected in GSTR-2B (auto-matched from suppliers' GSTR-1)
  - Under Rule 36(4): provisional ITC is capped at 105% of matched ITC (i.e., cannot claim more than 5% extra beyond what is verified in supplier filings)
  - Entities claiming ITC significantly in excess of their GSTR-2B — this is a primary fraud indicator

---

GSTR-2A / GSTR-2B: AUTO-POPULATED PURCHASE REGISTER

WHAT IT IS:
  - Auto-generated by the GSTN system based on suppliers' GSTR-1 filings
  - Shows every invoice a registered person received (from the supplier's perspective)
  - GSTR-2A: dynamic, updates as suppliers file or amend
  - GSTR-2B: static, generated on 14th of each month from GSTR-1 filed up to that date

FRAUD DETECTION RELEVANCE:
  - If entity claims ITC not reflected in its GSTR-2B, the ITC is unverified and potentially fraudulent
  - ITC mismatch ratio = (ITC_claimed - ITC_in_2B) / ITC_in_2B
  - Positive mismatch (claiming more than eligible) above 10% triggers scrutiny notice
  - Mismatch above 300% triggers immediate investigation

---

RULE 36(4) — PROVISIONAL ITC RESTRICTION

As per CGST Rule 36(4) (amended 2021):
  - Registered persons can claim ITC provisionally to the extent of 5% of eligible matched ITC
  - Formula: Provisional ITC allowed = Matched ITC (from GSTR-2B) × 105%
  - Any ITC claimed beyond this limit is now illegal and subject to reversal with interest

EXAMPLE:
  - Entity A has Rs. 100 Lakh matched ITC in GSTR-2B
  - Entity A can claim at most Rs. 105 Lakh in GSTR-3B (Rs. 100 Lakh matched + 5% provisional)
  - If Entity A claims Rs. 200 Lakh, the Rs. 95 Lakh excess is fraudulent ITC

AI SYSTEM SIGNAL: Tax mismatch ratio measures this excess. A ratio > 0.05 (5% buffer) is a compliance violation. A ratio > 1.0 (claiming 2x the eligible amount) is strong fraud evidence.

COMMON AUDIT RED FLAGS (Per CBIC Internal Audit Guidelines)

The following patterns automatically trigger Desk Scrutiny notices:
  1. ITC credit reversal for a return period but re-availing in subsequent months without matching supply
  2. ITC availment where supplier's registration was cancelled before the invoice date
  3. GSTIN mismatch — buyer GSTIN on invoice differs from the buyer's registered GSTIN
  4. Supply type mismatch — intra-state supply claimed as inter-state (to manipulate IGST credit)
  5. Excess ITC claimed beyond Rule 36(4) limits
  6. Multiple GSTR-1 amendments in the same period (suggests retrospective invoice creation)
  7. E-way bill not generated for goods movement above Rs. 50,000 (indicates no physical movement)
  8. Zero-rated (export) claims without corresponding ICEGATE (customs) records

GSTIN FORMAT VALIDATION RULES

A valid GSTIN: 29AAPFU0939F1ZV (15 characters)

Positions 1–2  : State code (01–37, UT codes included)
Positions 3–12 : PAN of the business (10 characters: 5 letters + 4 digits + 1 letter)
Position 13    : Entity number (1–9 for first entity, A–Z for subsequent entities under same PAN)
Position 14    : Letter 'Z' always (indicates GST registration under standard scheme)
Position 15    : Checksum digit (alphanumeric, computed from the preceding 14 characters)

VALID STATE CODES:
01=Jammu & Kashmir, 02=Himachal Pradesh, 03=Punjab, 04=Chandigarh, 05=Uttarakhand,
06=Haryana, 07=Delhi, 08=Rajasthan, 09=Uttar Pradesh, 10=Bihar,
11=Sikkim, 12=Arunachal Pradesh, 13=Nagaland, 14=Manipur, 15=Mizoram,
16=Tripura, 17=Meghalaya, 18=Assam, 19=West Bengal, 20=Jharkhand,
21=Odisha, 22=Chhattisgarh, 23=Madhya Pradesh, 24=Gujarat, 25=Daman and Diu,
26=Dadra and Nagar Haveli, 27=Maharashtra, 28=Andhra Pradesh (old),
29=Karnataka, 30=Goa, 31=Lakshadweep, 32=Kerala, 33=Tamil Nadu,
34=Puducherry, 35=Andaman and Nicobar Islands, 36=Telangana, 37=Andhra Pradesh (new)

FRAUD SIGNAL: Invalid GSTIN format indicates either data entry error or deliberately fake GSTIN in invoices.
